Europe is waking up to a harsh reality: its digital future is too dependent on American cloud services, chips, and artificial intelligence. A bold new plan, detailed by Les Echos, aims to break this reliance by investing billions into homegrown tech infrastructure.

The core of the strategy involves building sovereign cloud platforms that can rival Amazon, Google, and Microsoft. These European clouds will be designed to keep sensitive data within the continent's borders, a move that addresses growing privacy and security concerns.

On the hardware front, the plan pushes for a massive expansion of local chip fabrication plants. By funding new fabs and research hubs, Europe hopes to secure its own supply of advanced processors, which are currently dominated by American and Asian giants.

Artificial intelligence is the third pillar, with a focus on creating European AI models that compete with the likes of OpenAI and Google DeepMind. The initiative encourages collaboration between universities, startups, and industrial giants to develop ethical and transparent AI systems.

This ambitious project faces significant hurdles, including the sheer scale of investment needed and the risk of falling behind in the global AI race. However, the recent success of Chinese models like DeepSeek and Kimi shows that rapid innovation can come from outside the traditional US-centric ecosystem.

Ultimately, Europe's plan is not just about technology but about reclaiming digital sovereignty. If successful, it could reshape the global balance of power in cloud computing, chips, and artificial intelligence for decades to come.
