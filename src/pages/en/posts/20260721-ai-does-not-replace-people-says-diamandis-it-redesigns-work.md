In the latest episode of MOONSHOTS, Peter Diamandis hosts Salim Ismail for a debate that goes beyond the commonplace. The focus is not on replacing workers, but on reorganizing production processes.

According to Ismail, companies that achieve the greatest results with artificial intelligence are not laying off people. They are redesigning the way work is performed. The real gain, he argues, comes from rethinking entire workflows – not just automating isolated tasks.

The discussion touches on a central point of AIatolah's coverage: the real impact of AI on the job market. While many narratives still revolve around the fear of replacement, Ismail points to a more subtle and potentially more transformative path.

The video is relevant for those following trends in code agents, intelligent automation, and the future of work. The main message is that AI's competitive advantage lies in enabling human teams to operate at a higher level of abstraction and creativity.

For AIatolah, which covers the evolution of LLMs and hardware infrastructure, the message is clear: the value of technology lies not only in the models, but in the organizational architecture they enable.
