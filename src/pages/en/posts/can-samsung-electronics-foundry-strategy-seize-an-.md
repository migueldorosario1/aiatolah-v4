Samsung Electronics is betting big on its foundry strategy to challenge TSMC's dominance in the AI semiconductor era. The Korean giant aims to capture a slice of the market by offering a comprehensive package that includes memory, logic, and advanced packaging.

The company's aggressive push into Gate-All-Around technology aims to leapfrog TSMC in the next chip generation. However, TSMC's proven track record with major AI chip designers like Nvidia and AMD gives it a formidable edge in customer trust.

Samsung's unique advantage lies in its ability to integrate memory and logic chips on a single platform. This "one-stop shop" approach could appeal to AI firms seeking to reduce costs and improve performance.

Yet the foundry business requires massive capital investment and patience, as Samsung has learned from years of playing catch-up. The company must also overcome persistent yield issues that have deterred some major clients.

The rise of Chinese AI chip designers like DeepSeek and Cambricon presents a wildcard opportunity for Samsung. These firms are eager for a reliable alternative to TSMC, which faces geopolitical risks in Taiwan.

Samsung's foundry gambit is a high-stakes bet that could reshape the AI chip landscape. Success will depend on flawless execution and the ability to pivot faster than its Taiwanese rival.
